This poem was inspired by self-reflection on underlying issues, with myself. I had just started dating my wife and I held her in such awe of how amazing and mature she was and still is. I felt unworthy of her because I thought that I still had a lot of work left to do on myself. I felt childish and essentially needed a "rope" to save me from my old self's bad habits.

  • of his own shadowMy lover swears my sweet

  • I lie I hide another piece of mefrom
  • somebody who is supposed to keep me
  • whole. But time will only tellwhen it rains

  • lying right next to my ego. I've
  • swept this floor too many times to
  • tread in mudthat I no longer trust
  • because I found myself a rope in the

  • to bury myhead under my mother's
